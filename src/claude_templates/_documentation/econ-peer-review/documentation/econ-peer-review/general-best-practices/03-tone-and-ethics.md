# Tone and Ethics in Peer Review

> Guidelines for constructive, professional, and ethical reviewing.

## Be Constructive

- Criticize the paper, not the authors
- "The identification strategy does not account for X" is better than "The authors fail to consider X"
- Suggest fixes, not just problems. A review that only lists concerns without suggesting paths forward is less useful
- Frame criticism as opportunities: "The paper would be stronger if..." rather than "The paper is weak because..."

## Be Specific

- **Useful:** "The pre-trend at t=-3 is -0.184 (s.e. = 0.149), which is large relative to the ATT of 0.145. The authors should show robustness to excluding this cohort."
- **Not useful:** "The pre-trends are concerning."
- Reference specific pages, tables, figures, and equations
- Quote coefficient values and standard errors when discussing results
- Name the specific econometric concern (omitted variable bias, not "endogeneity")

## Be Honest About Uncertainty

- If you are not sure whether a concern is fatal, say so: "I am uncertain whether this threatens the main result, but the authors should address it"
- Do not reject a paper because of a concern you cannot articulate precisely
- Acknowledge when the paper handles a difficult problem well, even if imperfectly

## Be Proportionate

- A minor expositional issue is not a major concern. Do not inflate minor issues to justify a negative recommendation
- Conversely, do not bury a serious identification problem in the minor comments
- The length of your discussion of an issue should roughly match its importance
- One devastating identification problem can be more important than ten minor style issues

## Avoid

- **Self-citation demands.** Do not ask for citations to your own work unless genuinely relevant and you can articulate why
- **Scope expansion.** Do not ask the authors to write a different paper ("You should also study the effect on productivity")
- **Null-result bias.** Do not reject because results are null or unexpected. The credibility of the design, not the results, should drive the recommendation
- **Tone problems.** Never be dismissive, condescending, or personally critical. The review is about the work, not the people
- **Infeasible requests.** Do not request analyses that cannot be done with the available data. If you want to see something that requires different data, frame it as a limitation, not a requirement
- **Moving goalposts.** Do not introduce new demands in a second round that were not mentioned in the first round. Revision should converge, not expand

## The Golden Rule

Before submitting your review, ask: "Would I be comfortable receiving this review on my own paper?" If not, revise the tone.

## Ethics Reminders

- Maintain confidentiality. Do not share the paper, discuss it publicly, or use its ideas before publication
- Disclose conflicts of interest (co-authorship, shared affiliation, competitive work)
- If you cannot provide a fair review, decline the assignment
- Complete the review within the agreed timeline. If you need more time, notify the editor
- Do not use the paper's unpublished data or methods in your own research
