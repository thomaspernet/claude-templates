# Academic Writing Rules

## Citation

- Always cite: `(Author, Year)` inline or `Author (Year)` at sentence start
- Every claim not your own needs a citation
- Use `references.bib` for bibliography management
- Direct quotes: `> "text" (Author, Year, p. XX)`

## Argument Structure

Each section should follow: **Claim → Evidence → Analysis**

1. State your point clearly
2. Present supporting evidence with citations
3. Explain what the evidence means and why it matters

## Language

- Use hedging for uncertain claims: "suggests", "indicates", "appears to"
- Use strong language for established facts: "demonstrates", "shows", "confirms"
- Avoid absolutist language unless warranted: "always", "never", "proves"
- Define technical terms on first use

## Structure

- Introduction: context, gap, question, approach
- Body: organized by theme (not by source)
- Conclusion: summary, implications, limitations, future work

## Avoiding Plagiarism

- Paraphrase in your own words — don't just swap synonyms
- Cite even when paraphrasing
- Use quotes for distinctive phrases
- Your synthesis must add analytical value beyond summarizing
