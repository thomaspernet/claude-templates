# Research Methods Rules

## Source Evaluation

| Tier | Source type | Reliability |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | Peer-reviewed journals, official statistics | High |
| 2 | Books, reputable reports, conference papers | Medium-High |
| 3 | News articles, expert blogs, white papers | Medium |
| 4 | Wikipedia, forums, social media | Low — verify elsewhere |

## Search Strategy

- Document your search terms and databases used
- Record number of results and selection criteria
- Explain why sources were included or excluded
- Search at least 2 different databases/platforms

## Note-Taking

- One file per source in `notes/`
- Include metadata header (author, year, URL, access date, source type)
- Separate factual summary from your interpretation
- Record relevant direct quotes with page numbers
- Note connections to other sources

## Bias Awareness

- Check author affiliations and funding sources
- Look for conflicts of interest
- Note if findings are replicated elsewhere
- Be explicit about your own assumptions and perspective

## Triangulation

- Key claims MUST be supported by 2+ independent sources
- If only one source, mark as `[single source — verify]`
- Contradictions between sources MUST be noted and discussed
